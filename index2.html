Experimental Energy Interfaces

Social Energy Sensing Monument, or SESEME, is a public data visualization object. An embedded microcontroller receives energy data from networked buildings and changes its physical shape to reflect statistics. Hidden variable-color lighting, infrared sensors and an array of conditional behaviors will be used to make encounters with the object maximally responsive and emotive.

We are planning to use Bluetooth LE / iBeacon to communicate with nearby mobile devices, allowing users to access a web application that allows detailed exploration of data and physical manipulation of the device - a user could look for statistics from an hour ago, and both the web page and monument would change to display them.

The goal of the project is to create a social interface that can kindle conversations and influence perceptions, hopefully leading to more energy-conscious behavior. SESEME is a work in progress - I'm responsible for the industrial design and the interface design (both tangible and digital) and contributed to mechanical modeling and design. This is a project of the Consumer Energy Interfaces research group, where I work on energy feedback projects with a multidisciplinary team.

Mars Mission Design Animation

3D & 2D animation explaining design & progression for a 2018 mission to Mars, from spacecraft module selection to radiation shielding physics. Created in concert with a multidisciplinary team of students in aeronautical & mechanical engineering, physics, biology, etc. from UC Davis Eclipse Rocketry club. As a narrative video accompanying a 50-page proposal, helped the club become finalist in international mission design contest. Now being converted to a controllable visual presentation for final competition at the 17th Annual Int'l Mars Society Convention in Houston, TX (August 7-10, 2014).

five
Stories of Solidarity

Group research project investigating how social media can help working class laborers organize and build solidarity: we proposed a web based service that would lower barriers for workers with stories to tell by making submission easy across many platforms, from SMS to iOS. Stories would be displayed as map points with supplemental location-based data, allowing users to see labor activity and sentiment at different locations and scales, while learning more about the broader labor situation in any given area.

I participated in the hackathon where we formed the original concept, helping define visual identity and program the interactive mockup we presented at a UCLA labor symposium. Later, I rejoined the team to help present a much more refined, functional service at the 2013 AFL-CIO Convention, provided ideas for future development, and designed a large informational poster, on display now in labor research centers in Davis, Berkeley, and Vancouver.

157: Gamifying Campus Climate Control

Second-year interactive design project: social climate control and energy consumption feedback app. Members of a university community would use it provide feedback about air conditioning to building administrators, who could then make energy-saving or comfort improving adjustments accordingly. The design aims to make submitting feedback intuitive and easy while enabling and encouraging further exploration of energy usage data and the implications of A/C energy use. Small in-app rewards allow personalization of the experience and incentivize repeat usage. Comparing buildings and efficiency leaderboards introduce an element of social competition (i.e. engineers vs. artists vs. administrators) and community involvement / interest. Whimsical elements, like crowd-sourced "building announcements" or self-referential (UC Davis is known for its cow scent) graphics express an levity atypical of comparatively sterile utilities apps. back to index

37: Black Friday Simulator

An interactive faux-simulation, created for an intro to Processing class in my second year. Inspired by videos captured from various Wal-Marts on Black Friday, stories of Foxconn (Chinese manufacturer of electronic goods) workers commiting suicide, and the game design work of Jonatan Söderström (Hotline Miami, Norrland). Click to create goods and generate demand - consumers consume, workers work harder.
