Social Energy Sensing Monument

Social Energy Sensing Monument, or SESEME, is a public data visualization object. An embedded microcontroller receives energy data from networked buildings and changes its physical shape to reflect statistics. Hidden variable-color lighting, infrared sensors and an array of conditional behaviors will be used to make encounters with the object maximally responsive and emotive.

three
four

We are planning to use Bluetooth LE / iBeacon to communicate with nearby mobile devices, allowing users to access a web application that allows detailed exploration of data and physical manipulation of the device - a user could look for statistics from an hour ago, and both the web page and monument would change to display them.

The goal of the project is to create a social interface that can kindle conversations and influence perceptions, hopefully leading to more energy-conscious behavior. SESEME is a work in progress - I'm responsible for the industrial design and the interface design (both tangible and digital) and contributed to mechanical modeling and design. This is a project of the Consumer Energy Interfaces research group, where I work on energy feedback projects with a multidisciplinary team. back to index

180A: UC Davis Art Museum Design

Art museum design proposal & renderings, for institutional interior architecture class project. The design is formed from the intersection of a 30-60-90 and an equilateral triangle, with large columns functioning as structural support, marker of progress through the space, and solar chimneys.

Renders created with Mental Ray and Photoshop.

37: Black Friday Simulator

An interactive Processing sketch, created for an intro class in my second year. Inspired by videos captured from various Wal-Marts on Black Friday, stories of Foxconn (Chinese manufacturer of electronic goods) workers commiting suicide, and the game design work of Jonatan Söderström (Hotline Miami, Norrland). Click to create goods and generate demand - consumers consume, workers work harder.
